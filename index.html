Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about mortgages and our calculator.

A mortgage is a type of loan specifically used to purchase real estate. The property itself serves as collateral, meaning the lender can foreclose on it if you fail to make payments. Mortgages typically span 15–30 years and include both principal (the amount borrowed) and interest payments.
Your monthly payment is calculated using the formula: M = P[r(1+r)^n]/[(1+r)^n-1], where P is the loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate, and n is the number of payments. PITI payments include Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance.
PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is required when your down payment is less than 20% of the home's purchase price. It protects the lender, not you. PMI typically costs 0.5%–1.5% of the loan annually. You can request PMI removal when you reach 20% equity.
Key factors include: home price, down payment amount, interest rate, loan term, property taxes, homeowners insurance, PMI, HOA fees, and your credit score. A higher credit score typically qualifies you for lower interest rates, significantly reducing your payment.
Minimum down payments vary: Conventional loans require 3–20%, FHA loans require 3.5% (with 580+ credit score), VA loans require 0% for eligible veterans, and USDA loans require 0% for rural properties. A 20% down payment avoids PMI and reduces monthly payments.
Yes! Refinancing replaces your current mortgage with a new one, typically at a lower interest rate. It's generally worth it if you can lower your rate by at least 0.75–1%, plan to stay in the home long enough to recoup closing costs, and have good credit.

When to Refinance

Generally worthwhile if you can reduce your rate by at least 0.75% and plan to stay in the home long enough to recoup closing costs.

Watch Closing Costs

Refinancing typically costs 2–5% of the loan amount in closing costs. Make sure your savings exceed this amount before your planned move-out date.

Check Your Credit

A credit score of 740+ will get you the best refinance rates. Even small improvements to your score can save thousands over the loan life.
